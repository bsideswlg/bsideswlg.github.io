Call for Papers

Call For Papers (CFP) is open until August 26th! Go Submit!

In true Security B-Sides (BSides) form, BSides Wellington is a community security conference. And what better way to pay tribute to that community than putting consultants, hackers, hobbyists, academics, students, and public sector type folks in conference hall to share knowledge.

Submissions are reviewed by a CFP review board and will be accepted in 2 rounds; so if you have something to say, submit it now because the second round will be a bit less flexible in terms of time/format/topics as spots will be full.

Topics & Format

Talks can be focused on anything in the wider area of security: information, physical, hardware, wireless, application, beehive, personal, etc.

Here are a few ideas of talks or workshops just to get the ol’ brain juice flowing:

Technical talks (software or hardware related)

  • I broke something, look!
  • I broke something & then fixed it (i think)
  • I built something to stop other people from breaking things!
  • I built something to break other people’s things - automation yay!
  • Let’s build or break something together (workshop)

Research talks

  • I researched, studied, and tested something and this is the output!

Community talks

  • I started a project in the security community to do something - check it out!

Length

When you submit, you can decide on your talk length. Please don’t stretch material over a longer period just to fill time. The longer your talk, the higher the bar is set. We are happy to support anything from a 10-minute lightning talk or demonstration to a 60-minute workshop or talk.

Avoid this

We are not welcoming any vendor pitches or corporate cheerleading talks. You are welcome to talk briefly about what your company does, or use corporate slides; however your talk should be focused on sharing knowledge with the community, and not selling products and services.

Our code of conduct applies to all speakers, and we will not hesitate to blast “Flight of the Bumblebee” and usher you off stage if necessary.

If you made it this far through reading, go ahead and submit!

Go Submit!
